# Horology Vault — Competitive Landscape & Market Viability

## Bottom line

The market is real but not empty. There are at least a dozen actively-maintained watch-collection-tracking apps on the App Store alone, built almost entirely by solo indie developers, and the core feature set you'd expect (wear tracking, service history, market value, wishlist) has already become "table stakes" rather than a differentiator. That said, no single app has run away with the category — ratings volumes are small across the board, meaning nobody has "won" yet. Two features from your plan (the Fit Calculator and strap purchase recommendations with affiliate links) do not appear in any competitor found. Viability is real, but it depends on genuinely nailing those differentiators and execution quality, not on the category being underserved.

## Competitive landscape

### iOS App Store (the dedicated watch-tracker niche)

| App | Price model | Notable features | Traction signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **WatchGrid – Watch Tracker** | Free + $12.99 one-time Pro | Wear calendar, accuracy tracking, statistics dashboard (value, brand distribution), home screen widgets, "Meetup Mode" privacy toggle, iCloud/Dropbox sync, lug width field, manual market value tracking | 1 rating; very actively updated (near-weekly releases) |
| **Watch Collector** | Free + $3.99 one-time Pro | Rolex/Tudor focus, free CloudKit sync, market value tracking, accuracy tracking, wear usage tracking | 2 ratings |
| **Klokker** | Free (no paywall visible yet) | Wear rotation analysis with behavioral profiles ("The Faithful," "Active Enthusiast"), cost-per-wear, "Box Queens" neglect alerts, movement accuracy/health tracking, auto-generated monthly reports, wishlist, lug-width filter for straps, secure collection sharing | Approaching ~1,000 registered users per its own changelog — the most-established of the group |
| **Watch Collection Tracker** (Mehti Ozkan) | Free | Wear history, rotation suggestions, service history | 2 ratings; developer explicitly avoids adding "complex" features |
| **iCollect Everything** | Free tier + paid unlock/subscription | Not watch-specific — a generalist collectibles cataloger (also covers comics, coins, Funko Pops, etc.) with barcode scanning and cross-platform sync (iOS/Android/Mac/Windows/Web) | Established, many reviews, but reviewers note it feels generic rather than tailored to horology |

Also present in the category but not deep-dived: Horolog, Timeboxd, SOTC Watches, ChronoQueue (wishlist-focused), WristLog, Wroll, On My Wrist, Lugs, WristTime, iHorology, Winder, ChronoLog, Clockwork, TickTracer, Wristly, Zen Horology, Horologists, TimeWorld, Watch Box Collector, and **Bezelio** — a single-purpose watch *service log* app, which tells you service tracking alone has already been carved out as its own micro-niche.

### Google Play

Thinner than iOS. The dedicated options found (Watch Collection Tracker, Watch Collection Manager, WristTrack, My WatchBox Collection Gallery) look less polished than their iOS counterparts, and Chrono24 (a marketplace app) bolts on a basic "Watch Collection" feature rather than being purpose-built. Android is comparatively underserved — not directly relevant to your Apple-only V1, but worth knowing if you ever expand.

## What's now "table stakes," not a differentiator

Across nearly every competitor: wear tracking with a calendar/rotation view, service/accuracy history, manual market value tracking, wishlist, photo storage, and free cloud sync (iCloud/CloudKit). Your plan's Vault, Service History with accuracy chart, WearLog, Wishlist, and Provenance features all map directly onto this baseline — good validation that you scoped the right V1 feature set, but recognize these alone won't stand out against Klokker or WatchGrid, both of which already do this well and ship updates aggressively.

## What nobody else has (your real differentiation)

**The Fit Calculator** — comparing a watch's actual lug-to-lug measurement against the user's own wrist geometry to visualize fit. Klokker added a lug-width *filter* for matching straps, but no competitor found does geometric fit visualization against the user's wrist. This is a genuinely unclaimed feature.

**Strap recommendations with live pricing and affiliate purchase links** — no competitor offers a shopping/discovery feature for straps. The closest is Klokker's lug-width filter, which only searches the user's own existing strap inventory, not a retailer catalog. This is both a real differentiator and your clearest monetization angle beyond the app price itself.

## Pricing pattern — validates your plan

Every dedicated competitor with a paywall uses free-download-plus-one-time-unlock ($3.99–$12.99), not subscriptions. This strongly confirms the original instinct from your first brainstorm: collectors in this niche expect and respond to one-time pricing, and a subscription-only app would likely underperform on conversion relative to this norm. Your hybrid plan (one-time core, optional subscription for the live-data features) fits the observed market better than either pure model would.

## Risks worth naming plainly

This is not an empty field — it's a fragmented one, with several actively-maintained, well-executed competitors (WatchGrid and Klokker especially) already covering the baseline feature set well. Shipping "just another watch tracker" without the Fit Calculator and strap recommendations executed distinctly would mean competing on parity against apps that already have a head start and loyal small communities. The path to viability runs through those two differentiators actually being good, not through the category being underserved.

## Recommendation

Proceed, but treat the Fit Calculator and strap recommendation features as the reason the app exists, not as add-ons to a generic tracker — they're your only unclaimed ground. Everything else in the V1 plan (Vault, Service History, WearLog, Wishlist, Provenance) is necessary to be competitive but won't be why anyone chooses you over Klokker or WatchGrid.
